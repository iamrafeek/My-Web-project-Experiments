  Kollam

 

  Kollam, also known as Quilon, is a coastal city located in the southern state of Kerala, India. Situated on the banks of the Ashtamudi Lake and the Arabian Sea, Kollam has a rich cultural and historical heritage, dating back to the 9th century AD. It was a major trading hub for spices, especially black pepper, during the medieval period, and was an important center for the Chinese trade network. The city is also known for its beautiful backwaters, beaches, and scenic beauty, which attract tourists from all over the world. Kollam is also home to a number of temples, churches, and mosques, making it a multi-religious and multi-cultural city. Overall, Kollam is a fascinating destination for those interested in history, culture, and natural beauty.
